Full Syllabus

All classes meet on Wednesdays. Below is the complete overview.

Week Date (Wed) Chapter Topic Course Objectives SLOs Activities Assessments
Week 1 Jan 15 Course Syllabus Introduction, syllabus review Students can select interesting biological topics. Introduction, syllabus review
Week 2 Jan 22 The Scientific Method Basics of the scientific method and experimental design. Teach principles of the scientific method and hypothesis formation. Formulate hypotheses; identify variables. Discussion on students' selected topics, with the instructor demonstrating article selection and database use for references. The students practice searching articles related to the topic of interest.
Week 3 Jan 29 Finding Information About Topics in Science How to research and evaluate scientific sources. Develop research skills to find and assess credible sources. Locate and assess reliable scientific sources. Scavenger hunts for articles; compare sources. Quiz; submit experiment designs, evaluate sources, and submit bibliography.
Week 4 Feb 5 Reading and Writing About Statistical Analyses Understanding and writing about statistical data. Train students to interpret and write about statistics. Analyze and interpret statistical results. Analyze datasets and improve graph design. Write data analysis reports and graphs.
Week 5 Feb 12 Documenting Sources Proper citation and avoiding plagiarism. Teach proper citation practices and plagiarism prevention. Cite sources correctly; avoid plagiarism. Practice formatting citations; discuss plagiarism. Submit citation exercises; plagiarism response.
Students should annotate at least 5 articles, of which at least 2 articles will be focused on for presentation.
Week 6 Feb 19 Preparing a Scientific Paper Step-by-step guidance for scientific writing. Guide students to write structured scientific papers. Write clear, organized scientific papers. Create outlines and peer review sections. Draft paper; Quiz on paper structure.
Week 7 Feb 26 Data Analysis Using Statistics Statistical data analysis and presentation. Enable accurate analysis and presentation of statistical data. Perform and present statistical analyses. Select statistical tests and graph analysis. Submit statistical analyses and graphs.
Week 8 and 9 Mar 5 & Mar 12 Editing and improving scientific documents. Enhance revision skills for scientific writing. Revise drafts for clarity and accuracy. Edit texts; provide peer feedback. Submit revised drafts; use feedback checklists.
Week 10 Mar 19 Spring Break
Week 11 Mar 26 Poster Presentations Designing and presenting scientific posters. Teach effective poster design and presentation skills. Design and present scientific posters. Mock poster session; poster critique. Prepare and Submit posters to present at the end of this course, Quiz
Week 12 Apr 2 Oral Presentations Preparing and delivering oral presentations. Prepare students for professional oral presentations. Deliver structured, engaging oral talks. Three-minute thesis; presentation practice. prepare oral presentations; submit slides
Week 13-16 Apr 9, 16, 23 Student presentation
